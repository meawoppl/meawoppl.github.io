With WFH becoming hugely fashionable as a result of the [ongoing viral apocalypse](https://coronavirus.jhu.edu/map.html), I thought it would be a good time to share some tricks I learned in my experiments with [streaming myself programming](https://www.twitch.tv/meawoppl) on twitch, and **a hack that lets you use the tools in the streaming industry inside standard video conference software.**

Front and center in this the [Open Broadcaster Software](https://obsproject.com/) tool suite. It is a powerful cross-platform tool for screen recording, streaming, and real time video compositing. There are hundreds of tutorials out there that show you the [neat stuff](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wW4HiiksDcU) you can do with this platform. Here are some great jumping off points:

* [Getting started](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DTk99mHDX_I) (install, setup, basic scene editing)
* [RealSense type](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wW4HiiksDcU) background subtraction and overlay.
* [Background Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1mCuRFKoJg) and other silliness.
* [Complex scenes](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sEAM37uQvlY) and layered effects.

A demo with Image overlay, Multiple streamed windows and webcam

**Here is the fun part:** With an [extension to OBS](https://obsproject.com/forum/resources/obs-virtualcam.539/), ([or some extra linux/osx tricks](https://github.com/CatxFish/obs-virtual-cam/issues/17#issuecomment-345402851)) can output your “stream” to a virtual webcam, which is then usable by any/all video conferencing tools out there.
